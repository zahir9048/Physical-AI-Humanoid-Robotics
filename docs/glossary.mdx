# Glossary of Robotics and AI Terms

This glossary provides definitions for key terms used throughout the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook.

## A

**Action**: In ROS 2, a communication pattern for long-running tasks that provide feedback and can be canceled.

**Admittance Control**: A control strategy where the robot's motion is controlled based on applied forces, the inverse of impedance control.

**Algolia DocSearch**: A search solution commonly used in documentation sites to provide fast, relevant search results.

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.

## B

**Bipedal Locomotion**: The ability of a robot with two legs to walk or move in a stable manner.

**Bullet Physics**: An open-source physics engine used for simulating rigid body dynamics.

## C

**Capture Point (CP)**: A point used in bipedal robotics to determine where a robot needs to step to regain balance.

**Code Examples**: Detailed, runnable code snippets demonstrating concepts and techniques.

**Cognitive Planning**: High-level decision-making processes that enable robots to plan and execute complex tasks.

**Computer Vision**: A field of AI that enables computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world.

## D

**Dexterity**: The ability of a robotic system to perform fine manipulation tasks with precision.

**Dynamics**: The study of forces and torques that cause motion in mechanical systems.

**Docusaurus**: A static site generator for building documentation websites, used for this textbook.

## E

**Embodied Intelligence**: Intelligence that emerges from the interaction between an agent and its physical environment.

**End-Effector**: The tool or device at the end of a robotic arm that interacts with the environment.

**External Links**: References to resources outside the textbook, such as online tutorials or documentation.

## F

**Forward Kinematics**: The process of determining the position and orientation of the end-effector based on joint angles.

**Force Control**: A control strategy that regulates the forces applied by a robot to its environment.

## G

**Gazebo**: A 3D simulation environment for robotics that provides physics simulation and sensor models.

**Generative AI**: AI systems that can create new content, such as text, images, or other data.

**Grasping**: The ability of a robot to securely hold and manipulate objects.

**Gripper**: A device at the end of a robotic arm designed to grasp and hold objects.

## H

**Hardware Requirements**: Specifications for computational and physical components needed for robotics applications.

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)**: The study of interactions between humans and robots.

**Humanoid Robot**: A robot with a human-like body structure, typically featuring a head, torso, arms, and legs.

## I

**Impedance Control**: A control strategy that regulates the relationship between forces applied to a robot and its motion.

**Inverse Kinematics**: The process of determining joint angles required to achieve a desired end-effector position.

**Isaac SDK**: NVIDIA's robotics software development kit for developing and deploying AI-based applications.

**Isaac Sim**: NVIDIA's robotics simulation environment built on the Omniverse platform.

## J

**Joint Space**: The space defined by the joint angles of a robotic system.

## K

**Kinematics**: The study of motion without considering the forces that cause it.

## L

**Large Language Model (LLM)**: Advanced AI models capable of understanding and generating human-like text.

**Learning Objectives**: Clear statements that define what students should be able to do after completing a section.

**LiDAR**: Light Detection and Ranging, a remote sensing method using light in the form of a pulsed laser.

**Locomotion**: The ability of a robot to move from one place to another.

## M

**Manipulation**: The ability of a robot to interact with objects in its environment.

**Mermaid**: A diagramming and charting tool that uses markdown-inspired text definitions to render and modify diagrams dynamically.

**Motion Planning**: The computational problem of finding a sequence of movements for a robot to reach a goal.

## N

**Natural Language Understanding (NLU)**: A subfield of AI focused on enabling machines to understand and interpret human language.

**Navigation**: The ability of a robot to move through its environment while avoiding obstacles.

**Node**: In ROS, a process that performs computation. Multiple nodes communicate via topics, services, and actions.

## O

**Omniverse Kit**: NVIDIA's platform for 3D design collaboration and world simulation.

## P

**Path Planning**: The process of determining a route for a robot to follow from start to goal.

**Perception**: The ability of a robot to interpret sensory information from its environment.

**Physics Simulation**: Computer modeling of real-world physics to simulate interactions between objects.

**Practical Exercises**: Hands-on tasks that allow students to apply concepts learned in the textbook.

## R

**Real-time**: Systems that must respond to inputs within a guaranteed time frame.

**Reinforcement Learning (RL)**: A type of machine learning where agents learn to make decisions by receiving rewards.

**ROS 2 (Robot Operating System 2)**: A flexible framework for writing robot software, providing hardware abstraction and communication.

**Rviz2**: The 3D visualization tool for ROS 2.

## S

**Sensor Fusion**: The process of combining data from multiple sensors to achieve better accuracy and reliability.

**Sim-to-Real Transfer**: The process of transferring skills learned in simulation to real-world robots.

**SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**: The computational problem of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location.

**Speech Recognition**: The ability of a computer to identify and understand spoken language.

**Stereo Vision**: The process of extracting 3D information from camera images using multiple viewpoints.

## T

**Topic**: In ROS, a publish-subscribe communication pattern for streaming data between nodes.

**Trajectory Optimization**: The process of finding the optimal path for a robot to follow.

**TensorRT**: NVIDIA's inference optimizer and runtime for deep learning models.

## U

**URDF (Unified Robot Description Format)**: An XML format for representing robot models in ROS.

**USD (Universal Scene Description)**: NVIDIA's file format for 3D computer graphics applications.

## V

**Visual Perception**: The ability of a robot to interpret and understand visual information from cameras or other visual sensors.

**Voice-to-Action**: The process of converting spoken commands into robot actions.

## W

**Workspace**: The space in which a robot can operate or manipulate objects.

## Z

**Zero Moment Point (ZMP)**: A criterion used in bipedal robotics to determine the stability of a walking robot.
